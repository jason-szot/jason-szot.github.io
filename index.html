A Message from our founder:
Hunt County Pets Alive was started in March 2015 (we received our 501(c)(3) status in June 2015) so is a very young group but with big plans for the rescues and animals of Hunt County. Having been involved hands on with animal rescue for many years and just recently in this county, the need for a group to assist rescues in place became evident. Rescues are busy every day, not just on weekends trying to get animals adopted.

It's time consuming caring for the animals in foster, arranging transport, talking to people in need whether it be with a stray or injured animal, a family forced to rehome a pet, or making arrangements to pull a shelter baby as well as work full time and have a family life.

It's basically 24/7 and Hunt County Pets Alive would like to try to help with some of this burden by providing the following:

  • resources
  • information
  • workshops
  • communication among the groups
  • assisting with adoption events
  • providing forms and documents
  • assisting with websites and logos
  • other administrative type assistance
We will be looking into grants, feral cat programs (TNR ~ Trap, Neuter, Release) and a Barn Program.

I hope you will join us as there are many things that are needed and can be done from your own home, even if you can share a few minutes a day to assist. The rescues are the life savers of the animals, the voice of the voiceless. They need our support, and yours!

LD Garrison
Founder

At Your Service

T-N-R

We have a Trap Neuter Release program in Lone Oak, TX. We hope to work in other areas as we grow. This program helps keep the feral colony numbers to a minimum.

Volunteer ran

We are a foster-based rescue group. all our animals are kept in homes and around people.

Adoption

We sterilize all of our animals before adoption to keep the pet population under control.

We love our animals

We love all of our animals, and want to make sure they all get to go to their fur-ever homes.

Volunteering Opportunities!

Volunteer with HCPA

Volunteers are the soul of this and any animal advocacy group.
It is the volunteers that put their hearts and souls into saving the animals that have no voice.

Volunteers of HCPA have a variety of opportunities to help and to do so as their own time allows. Unlike a lot of rescues, HCPA does not actively seek volunteers to foster animals, tho if you desired to do so, we could certainly set you up to provide a loving, safe home until the animal finds a permanant one. Currently, volunteers are assisting with the cats located at Petco in Greenville by feeding and cleaning the cat habitats. There is also a need for volunteers to assist with the feeding and cleaning at the shelter in Greenville.

The internet... the opportunities are endless in the help needed to post on facebook and other avenues. If you prefer to not work directly with the animals, there are public relations opportunities, bookkeeping, form development and many other things that will greatly help our goal to save and assist the animals of Hunt County.

If you are considering becoming a volunteer, please complete the short online form so that we may visit with you further and keep you informed of our activities.
